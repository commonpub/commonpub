§ 03 — Gradient Descent
The Learning Rate Problem
Every neural network learns by making small adjustments to its weights. But how large should each adjustment be? Too small and training takes forever. Too large and the network overshoots and never converges.
Body Text

The learning rate η is the single most important hyperparameter to tune. It controls the step size taken in the direction of the negative gradient. Use the slider below to develop an intuition for its effects on the loss landscape.

Body Text

Notice how very small rates (η < 0.001) train correctly but slowly — the gradient steps barely move the weights. At very high rates (η > 0.05) the optimizer overshoots the minimum and the loss explodes, causing divergence.
